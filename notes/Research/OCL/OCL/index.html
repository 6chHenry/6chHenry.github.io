ITE Metrics

α, β Recognition: we measure the correctness of model prediction α^\hat{\alpha}α^ and β^\hat{\beta}β^​. For multi-label classification tasks, we use the mean Average Precision (mAP) metric.

Reasoning: we use Individual Treatment Effect (ITE) . ITEi=Yi,T=1−Yi,T=0ITE_i = Y_{i,T=1} - Y_{i,T=0}ITEi​=Yi,T=1​−Yi,T=0​ measures the causal effect T→YT \rightarrow YT→Y of ithi^{th}ith individual with the difference between outcomes ( YYY) with or without receiving the treatment ( TTT). In OCL, we discuss the causal relation between pthp^{th}pth attribute and qthq^{th}qth affordance: αp→βq\alpha_p \rightarrow \beta_qαp​→βq​. So we interpret the treatment TTT as the existence of αp\alpha_pαp​ and the outcome YYY as the βq\beta_qβq​ output. We measure the difference of βq\beta_qβq​ output when the whole αq\alpha_qαq​ feature is wiped out or not, which should be non-zero when the causal relation αp→βq\alpha_p \rightarrow \beta_qαp​→βq​ exists.

In detail, given a model, for an instance with causal relation αp→βq\alpha_p \rightarrow \beta_qαp​→βq​ ( p∈[1,114],q∈[1,170]p \in [1, 114], q \in [1, 170]p∈[1,114],q∈[1,170]), we first formulate ITE as the affordance probability change following Eq. 2:

ITE=Δβ^q=β^q∣do(αp)−β^q∣do(αq)ITE = \Delta \hat{\beta}_q = \hat{\beta}_q|_{do(\alpha_p)} - \hat{\beta}_q|_{do(\cancel{\alpha_q})}ITE=Δβ^​q​=β^​q​∣do(αp​)​−β^​q​∣do(αq​​)​

β^q∣do(αp)\hat{\beta}_q|_{do(\alpha_p)}β^​q​∣do(αp​)​ is the factual output of the affordance probability. β^q∣do(αq)\hat{\beta}_q|_{do(\cancel{\alpha_q})}β^​q​∣do(αq​​)​ is the counterfactual output when the αp\alpha_pαp​ is wiped out, which can be got by assign zero-mask [65] to the feature of αp\alpha_pαp​ (e.g., fαpf_{\alpha_p}fαp​​ in OCRN) and keep the other features. Then, based on ITE, we benchmark instances following:

ITE: If the causality αp→βq\alpha_p \rightarrow \beta_qαp​→βq​ exists on the instance, ITE should be non-zero when eliminating the effect of αp\alpha_pαp​. And the direction of ITE depends on the affordance ground-truth βq\beta_qβq​: if βq=0\beta_q = 0βq​=0, the predicted β^q\hat{\beta}_qβ^​q​ tend to be 1 after wiping out αp\alpha_pαp​ so ITE should be a negative value; contrarily, ITE should be positive if βq=1\beta_q = 1βq​=1. Hence we compute the ITE score as:

SITE={max⁡(Δβ^q,0),βq=1,max⁡(−Δβ^q,0),βq=0,S_{ITE} = \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \max(\Delta \hat{\beta}_q, 0), & \beta_q = 1, \\ \max(-\Delta \hat{\beta}_q, 0), & \beta_q = 0, \end{array} \right.SITE​={max(Δβ^​q​,0),max(−Δβ^​q​,0),​βq​=1,βq​=0,​

so that larger SITES_{ITE}SITE​ indicates the model infers more accurate ITE directions and has better reasoning performance.

α-β-ITE: we combine recognition and reasoning performances. We multiply SITES_{ITE}SITE​ with P(α^p=αp)P(\hat{\alpha}_p = \alpha_p)P(α^p​=αp​) and P(β^q=βq)P(\hat{\beta}_q = \beta_q)P(β^​q​=βq​) as a unified metric Sα−β−ITES_{\alpha-\beta-ITE}Sα−β−ITE​.

For all metrics, we compute AP for each [αp,βq][\alpha_p, \beta_q][αp​,βq​] and average them to mAP. Non-existing pairs are not considered.
